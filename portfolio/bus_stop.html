Modular Bus Stop Prototype

A bus stop created for the future of mobility

This bus stop was designed for a team of entrepreneurs working to improve mobility in Detroit, MI. The team was working on a challenge to improve mobility within the city. During their research they found that only 10% of city bus stops had benches and shelters. Furthermore, they learned that 40% of bus routes are proposed for changes. And, the reason that bus stops are lacking shelters? The city doesn't have the human capital keep up with the maintenance of current bus stops.

They proposed an innovative solution to this problem. To create a bus stop designed with molecularity in mind. A bus stop that is adaptable to changes and with parts that are interchangeable - making it easy to maintain. But, to bring their idea to life they needed a prototype to test and pitch their concept.

I designed and created a 3D design of this team's concept - helping them bring their idea to life. On this project I coordinated with the team to help them build an initial design in SketchUp. I then worked with them on several revisions of the design to bring it to its final iteration. I then uploaded the model online to a cloud-based platform, making it available to their full use.
